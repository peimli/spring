About the Cherry Blossom Festival

The Cherry Blossom Festival is an annual event that celebrates the blooming of cherry blossom trees in Japan. The festival typically takes place in late March or early April, depending on when the cherry blossoms are predicted to be in full bloom.

During the festival, visitors can enjoy picnics under the cherry blossom trees, participate in traditional Japanese activities, and attend cultural events and performances.

Events

  • Sakura viewing parties
  • Traditional Japanese tea ceremonies
  • Street food vendors
  • Cultural performances, including taiko drumming and traditional dance
  • Fireworks displays
  • Art exhibits featuring cherry blossom-inspired artwork
  • And much more!

Location

The Cherry Blossom Festival takes place in various locations throughout Japan, depending on when the cherry blossoms are predicted to be in full bloom. Some popular locations include:

  • Ueno Park in Tokyo
  • Kyoto Gosho (Imperial Palace) in Kyoto
  • Hirosaki Castle in Aomori
  • Himeji Castle in Hyogo

For more information on the festival locations and schedules, please visit the Japan National Tourism Organization's website.
